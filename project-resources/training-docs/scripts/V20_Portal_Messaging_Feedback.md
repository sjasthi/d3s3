# V21 — Patient Portal: Messaging & Feedback

**Phase:** 6 — Patient Portal  
**Audience:** Patients  
**Estimated duration:** ~3 minutes  
**Watch first:** V19  
**Prerequisites for viewer:** Has watched V19 (Getting Started)

---

## Production Setup

- **Login as:** Portal test patient (seed data — patient with at least one existing message thread, to show both composing and an existing conversation)
- **Start screen:** Patient portal dashboard
- **Test data needed:** Existing message thread in seed data. Active portal account.
- **Note:** Show the full compose → send → view reply flow if possible. The thread-based UI should feel familiar (similar to email). Feedback is a separate flow from messaging — cover both clearly.
- **Tone:** Friendly and empowering — this is how patients advocate for themselves and communicate with their care team.

---

## Script

### [0:00–0:10] Opening
> [On screen: portal dashboard]

"Your patient portal includes two ways to communicate with the clinic — messaging and feedback. Let me walk through both."

---

### [0:10–0:40] Messages — overview
> [Action: Click "Messages" in the top nav]

> [On screen: portal/messages.php — inbox view]

"The Messages section works like a simple inbox. You'll see any conversations you've already had with the clinic here. If there are unread replies from your care team, they'll appear highlighted."

---

### [0:40–1:15] Starting a new conversation
> [Action: Click "New Message" or "Compose" button]

> [On screen: compose form — subject and message body]

"Click New Message to start a conversation. Give it a subject so the clinic team can quickly understand what it's about."

> [Action: Type subject: "Question about my prescription"]

"Then write your message. Be as specific as you can — include dates, symptom descriptions, or medication names so the team can respond accurately."

> [Action: Type message: "Hello, I was prescribed Paracetamol at my last visit. I wanted to ask — is it safe to take this alongside the iron supplements I'm already taking? Thank you."]

> [Action: Click Send]

> [On screen: message sent confirmation or inbox updated]

"Your message is sent. The clinic's staff will see it and reply — usually within one business day."

---

### [1:15–1:40] Viewing a reply
> [Action: Open an existing thread with a staff reply]

> [On screen: message thread — patient message and staff reply side by side]

"When the clinic replies, you'll see a notification badge on the Messages icon. Open the thread to read their response and continue the conversation."

> [Action: Type a brief reply in the reply field]

"You can reply directly in the same thread — no need to start a new message for a follow-up question."

---

### [1:40–2:10] Feedback — submitting a concern or suggestion
> [Action: Click "Feedback" in the top nav]

> [On screen: portal/feedback.php]

"The Feedback section is for submitting formal concerns, complaints, or suggestions about your care or experience at the clinic. This goes to the clinic's Grievance Officer."

> [Action: Click "Submit Feedback" or "New Submission"]

> [On screen: feedback form — category dropdown, description field]

"Select the type of feedback — complaint, compliment, or suggestion — and write a clear description of what you'd like the clinic to know."

> [Action: Select category: "Complaint". Type description: "I waited over 90 minutes for my appointment without any update from staff."]

> [Action: Click Submit]

---

### [2:10–2:30] What happens after you submit
> [On screen: feedback list — submitted item with status "Open"]

"Your submission is logged and assigned to the Grievance Officer. You can see its status here — Open, In Progress, or Resolved. The clinic will follow up with you directly to address the concern."

---

### [2:30–3:00] Closing
"Messaging and feedback together give you a direct channel to your care team and to clinic management. Use messaging for clinical questions, and feedback for anything related to your overall experience. Both are private and secure."
